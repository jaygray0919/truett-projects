New Video of Alec Baldwin's 'Rust' Shows What Happened on Set

Investigators release footage of rehearsals, text messages and interviews with witnesses of the shooting on set

By Erich Schwartzel
Updated Apr. 26, 2022
Tap story

While preparing for a scene on the low-budget Western “Rust,” which was being filmed near Santa Fe, N.M., Alec Baldwin discharged a live round from a revolver, killing Halyna Hutchins, the 42-year-old cinematographer. The film’s director, Joel Souza, was wounded.

On Monday the Santa Fe County Sheriff’s Office released footage from their investigation into the “Rust” shooting, in response to public-records requests by media outlets. It includes body-camera footage from deputies responding to the incident and interviews with key witnesses. Here’s what happened that day:

The investigation is ongoing, but the trove of information released Monday sheds new light on the hours and days immediately following the shooting.

Ms. Gutierrez Reed’s lawyer, Jason Bowles, on Monday pointed to a New Mexico workplace safety inspectors' report that found Ms. Gutierrez Reed wasn’t given authority to determine whether gun training was needed or time to thoroughly inspect ammunition. He has previously said no one on set told Ms. Gutierrez Reed that Mr. Baldwin was doing an impromptu scene rehearsal with the gun.

Mr. Baldwin’s lawyer, Luke Nikas, said Monday that Mr. Baldwin welcomes this investigation and that the new releases show he acted responsibly.

Mr. Halls’s lawyer, Lisa Torraco, has previously rejected the assertion that her client handed the gun to Mr. Baldwin.

Ms. Mitchell referred a request for comment to her attorney.

Rust Movie Productions has not responded to past requests for comment on allegations of an unsafe workplace.
