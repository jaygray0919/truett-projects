Take Control

We are surrounded by advice about how to live our lives.

Problems with printed advice

But there are problems with this approach.

Instructions are generalized for many; not customized for a person with special needs.

We have no easy way to store information printed on a label or insert.

And what about intake and exposure data over days, weeks, months or years?

Opportunity

Our opportunity is to combine a useful concept ("advice from experts") with real data related to advice.

If we systematically capture and organize intake and exposure data, experts (our doctors, trainers, nutritionists) can analyze our data over time.

Then life style changes - ones we choose or are proposed by advisers - are based on a personalized baseline.

It's not just what we do (or don't do) on a given day; it's what we have done (or haven't done) over time.

Biograph

To meet the opportunity Ontomatica developed Biograph.

Biograph collects data about food and drugs you consume, and chemicals in cosmetics and personal care products you use.

It stores data over days, weeks, months, and years.

It is your electronic biography of intake and exposure during your lifetime.

Like your health records, it's data your advisers study to personalize their advice and care.

Biograph builds on two technologies. The first is Ontomatica's Graph Kits.

Graph Kits define "things" (items; their properties; their relationships with other items).
Graph Kits are "collections of things" in foods, drugs, supplements, herbal medicines, cosmetics, personal care products, and household chemicals.

Biograph then uses W3C standards and technology from Solid 'link' to track "things".

Solid

Solid offers a new way of building applications (apps).

Rather than storing your data inside an app that requests it, you store data in your own personal data pod.

Here's a way to think about it.

The JPG standard makes it possible to create, modify, share and store images.

It's your asset; you control its details; you decide who can see it.

Solid aims to implement the JPG model for documents, and for data inside documents.

Solid and Biograph

So when you give an app permission to access data in your Biograph, the app will reuse data fields such as:

your name
food consumed (item)
drug consumed (item)
calendar (date/time) when you consumed an item
place where you consumed an item
link to consumed item that shares biology, chemistry, composition, and formulation data about the item
link to consumed item that shares origin, preparation and packaging data about the item
link to consumed item that shares route-of-administration data about the drug item
and more

Linked Data from Graph Kits is the staring point.

Biograph then integrates three complementary technologies from Solid.

Summary

Ontologies define facts about things and relationships among things.

Graph Kits use ontologies to define things that are closely related.

Products and product components are defined using Graph Kits and presented using Electronic Labels.

Electronic Labels provide intake data for your Biograph.

Biograph uses Shapes to link intake data with outcome data (sensor or monitor data).

Biograph uses Forms to present intake and outcome data to users and advisers.

Biograph uses Footprints to store data in a pod that you control.

Using that data, you adjust your lifestyles based on your personal choices or expert advice.

But you control the data and share it on your terms.

Biograph puts you in charge of making data intensive decisions and sharing your data with apps and people you trust.
